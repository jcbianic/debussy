Jazz piano is a style of piano playing rooted in African American musical traditions that emerged in New Orleans in the early 20th century. It encompasses a wide range of techniques and substyles, including stride, bebop, and modal jazz, each characterized by distinct rhythmic and harmonic approaches. Pioneering figures such as Jelly Roll Morton, Thelonious Monk, and Bill Evans shaped the language of jazz piano by expanding harmonic vocabulary and rhythmic complexity. The instrument plays a dual role in jazz ensembles, functioning both as a melodic voice and a harmonic anchor, often comping behind soloists while also taking extended improvisational solos. Today, jazz piano remains a central pillar of jazz education and performance worldwide.
